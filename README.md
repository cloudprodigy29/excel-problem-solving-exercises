# Excel Problem Solving Case Study

A collection of spreadsheet-based business case studies demonstrating practical Excel skills in data modeling, financial analysis, formula design, and data visualization.

---

## Overview

This repository contains my solutions to a series of Excel problem-solving exercises designed to evaluate analytical thinking rather than simply spreadsheet proficiency. Each scenario requires interpreting a real-world problem, identifying the necessary calculations, building a dynamic spreadsheet model, and presenting the results through charts that support decision-making.

Rather than performing manual calculations, every solution was designed using formulas so that changes to the underlying data automatically update the final recommendations.

These exercises showcase my ability to transform unstructured business requirements into organized, scalable spreadsheet models.

## Case Studies Included

### 1. School Shopping Price Comparison

**Objective**

Determine the most cost-effective store for two different shoppers (Susan and Tim) based on their shopping lists.

**Skills Demonstrated**

* Lookup and pricing calculations
* Quantity-based cost modeling
* Comparative analysis across multiple vendors
* Bar chart visualization

---

### 2. Cat vs. Dog Cost of Ownership

**Objective**

Calculate and compare the total first-year cost of owning either a cat or a dog by accounting for one-time purchases and recurring monthly expenses.

**Skills Demonstrated**

* Annual cost projections
* Recurring expense calculations
* Total cost of ownership analysis
* Cost comparison visualization

---

### 3. Vacation Cost Comparison

**Objective**

Determine the least expensive vacation option by considering airfare, lodging, transportation, food, and attraction costs for different family sizes.

**Skills Demonstrated**

* Multi-variable financial modeling
* Per-person cost calculations
* Scenario analysis
* Comparative charts

---

### 4. Printer Total Cost of Ownership

**Objective**

Compare printer options by evaluating both the purchase price and long-term operating costs based on projected printing volume.

**Skills Demonstrated**

* Lifetime cost analysis
* Variable operating expense calculations
* Cost forecasting
* Business decision modeling


---

### 5. Vehicle Cost of Ownership

**Objective**

Compare the lifetime ownership cost of three vehicles by incorporating purchase price, financing assumptions, fuel efficiency, insurance, taxes, licensing, and operating costs.

**Skills Demonstrated**

* Comprehensive financial modeling
* Lifetime ownership analysis
* Multi-factor cost comparison
* Decision-support reporting

## Spreadsheet Design Principles

Each worksheet was designed with readability and maintainability in mind:

* Organized input and calculation sections
* Formula-driven calculations (minimal hard-coded values)
* Consistent formatting
* Clearly labeled assumptions
* Dynamic outputs
* Charts summarizing recommendations
* Easy-to-follow workbook structure
